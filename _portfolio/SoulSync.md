## Heading 
Led the Narrative team which focused on developing Story, Dialogue, and Cutscenes. Occasionally worked on tasks for Art team when needed. Composed some of the soundtrack and worked on the audio team.
 SoulSync was The University of Tulsa's Computer Simulation and Gaming Major's Senior Class Project. The goal was to create a game over the course of an entire academic year, the only requirements for the game were 1. The game had to have 4 levels, and 2. The game needed to have multiplayer. The game we decided on was a Fantasy-Cyberpunk Roguelike. This has been the most intense project I have ever worked on in my life, but it has also been the most enlightening. The game has a total of 13 developers working on it (Not including the audio team which was technically another class), the largest team I had ever worked on. Communication was really rough at first, there was certainly an issue with a few people speaking louder than everyone else and trying force us to make the game they wanted to make. But after weekly meetings for a few months we managed to build a culture where everyone could have their voice heard. The class was initially divided into three groups, Programming, Art/Design, and Narrative. Programming and Art had an equal number of developers, 5 for each, but Narrative was much smaller only having 3 members including myself. The other members of the Narrative team had an extremely full schedule that semester so I decided to step up and become the team lead. This meant that in addition to the team wide meetings I would also be attending team lead meetings for half an hour before every class.
