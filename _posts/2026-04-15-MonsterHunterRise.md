  Monster Hunter Rise is the 2nd game in 5th generation of Monster Hunter Games, following up the very popular Monster Hunter: World. Rise was my first introduction to the series. I completed the low rank initially, and after beating Monster Hunter Wilds I decided to come back and beat the high rank story. I forgot how fun the mechanics are. Being able to zip around with the wirebugs and pull off crazy moves is really engaging. The monster designs and choices are also extremely good, containing what I believe to be some of if not the best designs in the series (it also has my favorite monster, you'll never guess which one :D). Rise has a traditional Japanese theme and as such both the new and returning monsters fit into the theme of Japanese Yo-Kai. The first monster you fight in the game, Great Izuchi is based upon the Kamaitachi a weasel with a sickle tail. Another Monster in the game, Aknosom, is based on the Kasa-Obe, a Yo-Kai that is a living parasol with one big eye. My favorite is of course Almudron, based on the Dorotabō; The vengeful ghosts of old men come back as apparitions made of mud. This is pretty apparent in Almudron's design,  big eyebrows that are permanently fixed in a scowl, long whiskers and a temperamental attitude. He's a strange mix of a lobster, cat, dragon, and old man. He throws mud at you and his armor turns you into a kick-ass robot. He's awesome and I love him. Next is the flagship monster of the game, Magnamalo. It's design is that of a ghost samurai. He summons balls of ghostly mist which explode into balls of fire. He's a mostly forgettable flagship compared to ones from other games, that is until you reach the final boss of the game. The final boss of the game is a fight between two monsters, Wind Spirit Ibushi and Thunder Spirit Narwa, representing the Japanese gods of wind and thunder; Raijin and Fujin. The fight has you battle Ibushi first; and once you defeat him you fall down from the arena into a cavern with Narwa, she consumes Ibushi's body and transforms into All Mother Narwa, combining both their mastery of wind and lighting. About halfway through this already awesome fight, Magnamalo descends into the arena and FIGHTS NARWA WITH YOU (A new feature introduced in this game is Wyvern riding, where you can mount monster and use briefly use them to fight other monsters). And once this happens Narwa's theme cuts out and is replaced with the main theme of the series, Proof of a hero. This results in a final boss fight that I personally place in the top 10 final bosses of all time. This game is a gem. It was criticized on release for not being Monster Hunter: World 2, but I think that's ok. World was a great game with its own issues (Oops all wyverns) and instead of trying to chase that high like many developers do, Capcom decided to try something different with Rise and wait until that had more to build on for a successor to World. Playing through Rise was an amazing experience and I can't wait to play through the Master Rank expansion Monster Hunter Rise: Sunbreak.
